Artemis

Artemis was the goddess of:

  • The Hunt
  • The Forests
  • The Hills
  • The Moon
  • and Archery

Artemis was one of the most regarded of the Greek Goddesses. Her Roman Equivalent is most likley Diana. Her history is seen as the daughter of Zeus and Leto, so this makes here the twin sister of Apollo. Artemis is widley regarded as the Goddess of the Hunt, Forests and Hills, the Moon, and the Goddess of Archery. Her childhood was not recorded in any form. The Illiad claims that she was thrashed by Hera, so she climbed weeping into Zeus's lap. Artemis was seen a lot thorughout Greek Culture. On the right, you can see a coin that was inscribed with her likeness.
In addition to having coins made in her likness, Artemis was also widley worshipped and celebrated. The Artemis Temple was built for this purpose. In additon to this, there was also the Artemis Sanctuary. These two things were built to worship her. There were also many festivals that were in her dedication. In the end, Artemis was one of the most influential Greek Gods. She was remembered as the Goddess of the Hunt, and her countless temples, sanctuaries, and events and festivals will remember her for this.
